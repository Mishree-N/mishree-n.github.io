About Me

Welcome! I am Mishree Narasaiah, a freshman at UNC pursuing a Neuroscience, B.S. degree on the pre-medical path. Post-graduation, I hope to attend an MD/PhD program, where I plan to continue pursuing my commitment to scientific research and medicine. Having previously lived in India, Denmark, Singapore, and California, I came to Chapel Hill from my most recent home in Austin, Texas. In Austin, I completed the International Baccalaureate Diploma Program (IBDP), exploring my esoteric interests in science and research as part of my coursework.

Specifically, for my long-term research project in biology, I conducted research on the most efficacious contact lens cleaning solution in order to prevent bacterial eye infections caused by Staphylococcus epidermidis that can manifest as a result of improper usage and cleaning. Consequently, I gained the skill of research design by crafting my own study, learned how to implement the aseptic technique, and wrote my first full research paper outlining my findings. As a result of this experience, I was able to submit my paper to the International Young Researchers’ Conference, a program hosted by Columbia University. After learning about my manuscript’s acceptance, I also designed a scientific poster to present at Columbia University in December 2024.

Additionally, as part of the IBDP, I conducted independent research in behavioral biology, working with Otala lactea and Bulimulus guadalupensis, two species of snails, in a maze-navigation experiment I crafted to understand pheromone-based communication via mucin trails. As a result, I gained experience working with live organisms in an ethical manner, and I was also able to refine my research paper writing skills. For further details on my research experiences and publications, please visit my Research and Publications page!

Moreover, in Austin, I explored my interests in the healthcare field through various internships at UT Austin’s medical school and its associated clinics. Specifically, I interned at UT Health Austin's Mitchel and Shannon Wong Eye Institute, where I discovered my interest in neuro-ophthalmology, a field I am hoping to pursue as a physician. Working with a variety of patients in such clinical settings also helped me reinforce my desire to pursue a medical degree. Specifically, viewing the doctors I shadowed interact with their patients to create a diagnosis and then treat them with tact and empathy confirmed that I hope to be in that position one day.

Currently, at UNC, I am involved in research at the Hantman Lab in the Neuroscience Center of UNC’s medical school, where I research the neural mechanisms of skilled motor control post-stroke recovery in mice using optogenetics. Finally, I am a member of Remote Area Medical (RAM), an organization that works to provide free healthcare clinics to help meet the medical, dental, and vision needs of underserved communities. Specifically, I am on their Community Host Group committee, where I am working towards establishing a local clinic in Chapel Hill with physicians and student volunteers on site. Our goals are to fundraise, network with physicians willing to volunteer their time, and communicate with RAM headquarters to achieve our longitudinal goal by 2027.
